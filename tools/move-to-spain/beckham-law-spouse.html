🇪🇸 Beckham Law for Spouse

What This Is

Since the 2023 reform (Ley 28/2022, "Ley de Startups"), the spouse of a Beckham Law holder can opt into the same special tax regime (Art. 93 LIRPF). The application is Modelo 149, which is electronic-only (Order HFP/1338/2023). This guide walks through every step.

Deadline: ~June 29, 2026. Filed within 6 months of her entry into Spain (Dec 29, 2025). In practice AEAT takes 2-8 weeks to process, so start now. The 6-month deadline is absolute — the Spanish Supreme Court (May 2020) confirmed even one day late means permanent denial.
Your 149 must be approved first. AEAT requires the principal taxpayer's Modelo 149 to be filed before the spouse's. Her form requires your M149 justification number (Field 11). If yours is already approved, you're good.

Eligibility requirements

  • Not been Spanish tax resident in the prior 5 tax years
  • The sum of taxable bases (bases liquidables) of all associated family members is lower than yours (checked annually)
  • She is a Spanish tax resident (lives in Spain)
  • She does not need to be employed — employment conditions apply only to the principal

Recommended Order of Approaches

There are two things to do: (1) Modelo 030 (register in the tax census — paper form, required first) and (2) Modelo 149 (the actual Beckham Law application — electronic only). Here's how to get both done, ranked from best to fallback.

RankStrategyWhy
#1 Hire a gestor / asesor fiscal 200-500 EUR. They handle everything (030 + 149 + doc upload) as a colaborador social. Zero digital access needed from her. Given 2 months left on the deadline, this is the safest bet.
#2 AEAT visit for 030 + get her certificado digital at same visit Free. File 030 on paper, then do FNMT identity verification at the same AEAT office (most are accreditation offices). She can file 149 herself online the same day.
#3 AEAT visit for 030 + register you as apoderado Free. File 030, then register apoderamiento so you can submit 149 from home with your cert. Good if getting her a cert is too fiddly.

If she's never worked in Spain or filed taxes, she's not in AEAT's system. Modelo 149 will error out without this. Unlike 149, the 030 can be submitted on paper.

Steps

  1. Download: Modelo 030 PDF (812 KB)
  2. Fill it out (see field guide below)
  3. Book cita previa: AEAT Cita Previa or call 91 333 5 333 (Mon-Fri, 9-19h). Procedure: Censo, NIF y domicilio fiscal
  4. Go to office with: printed 030 (2 copies), passport (original + copy), NIE / Certificado de Registro

or do it yourself
▶
#2 Best DIY approach
Get her digital access (Cl@ve or certificado digital), then submit 149 online
Requires some setup Free Useful forever

She needs one of: Cl@ve, certificado digital (FNMT), or DNIe (Spanish nationals only — not applicable). Two realistic paths:

Path A: Cl@ve via postal invitation letter (easiest)

After filing Modelo 030 (which registers her in the tax census), AEAT can mail a Cl@ve activation letter to her Spanish address.

  1. File Modelo 030 first (Step 1 above)
  2. Go to clave.gob.es registration → choose "Carta de invitacion" (invitation letter)
  3. Enter her NIE + date of expiry from her NIE document
  4. AEAT mails a letter with a verification code to her registered tax address (from the 030)
  5. Letter takes a few days to arrive — use the code to complete Cl@ve registration online
  6. Submit Modelo 149 at the AEAT portal using Cl@ve
Why this is easiest: No software to install, no office visit beyond the 030, works from any browser. The only downside is waiting a few days for the letter.

Path B: FNMT certificado digital (try at the same AEAT visit)

Most AEAT offices are also FNMT accreditation offices. If you can get the online request done beforehand, you can do the identity verification while you're there for the 030.

Caveat: The FNMT online request is not as simple as filling in a web form. You must first install the FNMT-RCM Configurador software (available for Windows, macOS, Linux). The form asks for her NIE number (9 characters, zero-padded), first surname, and email. She gets an activation code by email. The cert must later be downloaded on the same device + browser used for the request. macOS/Safari has known compatibility issues — use Firefox or Chrome.
  1. Before the visit: Install FNMT Configurador from sede.fnmt.gob.es. Then go to "Solicitar certificado" → enter her NIE, surname, email. Save the activation code from email.
  2. At the AEAT office (after filing 030): Ask for FNMT identity accreditation. Bring:
    • Her passport (original) — proves identity
    • Her NIE document (Certificado de Registro, TIE, or resolucion favorable) — proves NIE
    • The activation code from the email
  3. At home: Download the cert from FNMT (same browser + device). Export the .p12 file with a password as backup.
  4. Submit Modelo 149 using the cert.

Alternative: FNMT offers video identification for 2.99 EUR (24/7) instead of in-person accreditation.

▶
#3 Free alternative
Register you as her apoderado (representative), you submit 149
1 office visit + online filing Free

She goes to AEAT to grant you power of attorney. Then you file 149 from home with your own certificado digital.

At the AEAT office (same visit as 030)

  • After filing 030, ask to register an apoderamiento
  • She signs the Anexo I form (staff provide it) naming you as representative
  • Bring: her passport + NIE, your NIF/NIE
  • Both of you should attend (some offices verify the apoderado's identity too)
  • Alternative: notarized power of attorney (poder notarial) from a notario (~50-100 EUR)
Note: The apoderamiento may take 24-48 hours to appear in AEAT's system. Don't try to file 149 the same day — wait for confirmation.

From home

  • Log in to AEAT Modelo 149 portal with your certificado digital
  • Upload docs via "Aportar documentacion"
  • Submit Modelo 149 on her behalf
What to say

"Vengo para registrar un apoderamiento. Quiero autorizar a mi marido como representante para tramites y actuaciones en materia tributaria en mi nombre. Aqui esta su NIF: [your NIF]."

(I'm here to register a power of attorney. I want to authorize my husband as representative for tax procedures on my behalf. Here's his NIF.)

or
▶
#4 Hail Mary
Ask the AEAT officer to submit 149 for her at the office
No guarantee Free

AEAT offers "asistencia en la confeccion" for some forms (mainly Renta). The idea: after filing 030, ask the officer to also submit 149 electronically. Zero documented cases of this working for Modelo 149 specifically. It's entirely at the officer's discretion.

  • Better chance at smaller/suburban offices
  • Bring all documents + data pre-prepared so the officer only types
  • If they say no, immediately pivot to apoderamiento registration (Approach #3) or FNMT cert (Approach #2)
Script

"Ya esta dada de alta en el censo. Necesitamos presentar el Modelo 149 para acogerse al regimen especial de impatriados como conyuge. Es solo electronico y no tiene acceso digital. ¿Pueden ayudarnos a presentarlo?"

(She's now in the census. We need to submit Modelo 149 for the impatriados regime as a spouse. It's electronic-only and she has no digital access. Can you help us submit it?)

If they say no — pivot

"Entendido. ¿Podemos entonces registrar un apoderamiento para que yo pueda presentarlo en su nombre?"

(Understood. Can we register a power of attorney so I can submit it on her behalf?)
