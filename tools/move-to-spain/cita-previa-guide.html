🇪🇸 Cita Previa Guide

Booking a Toma de Huellas Appointment (TIE Fingerprinting)

After your EX-19 or residence authorization is approved, you need a toma de huellas (fingerprinting) appointment to receive your physical TIE card. Getting this appointment in Madrid is notoriously difficult due to bots and high demand.

Key Discovery (April 2026): The "sin Cl@ve" (without digital certificate) path often shows zero availability because bots exhaust those slots. However, the "con Cl@ve" path frequently has appointments available. If you or someone you know has Cl@ve, use that path.

The Cl@ve Workaround

The Problem

When you try to book via the "sin Cl@ve" path, you'll likely see:

"En este momento no hay citas disponibles para la reserva sin cl@ve."
Followed by: "SI TIENEN A SU DISPOSICION MEDIANTE EL USO DE CL@VE, CITAS DISPONIBLES PARA SU RESERVA."

Translation: No appointments without Cl@ve, but yes there are appointments available via Cl@ve.

The Catch-22

To register for Cl@ve, you typically need a numero de soporte from your TIE card. But you need the fingerprinting appointment to get the TIE card. Circular dependency.

The Solution: Someone Else Can Book For You

The official portal explicitly states: "Si todavia no disponen de acceso al certificado electronico, pueden solicitar la cita a traves de cualquier entidad o persona que disponga de el, y podran reservar la cita en su nombre."

Translation: Anyone with Cl@ve can book the appointment on your behalf. The Cl@ve authenticates the booker, not the applicant.

Who can help:

  • Your spouse/partner if they already have Cl@ve (e.g., the EU citizen sponsor who already has their TIE)
  • A friend or colleague in Spain with Cl@ve access
  • A gestoria or immigration lawyer -- this is why they can get appointments when you can't (they have Cl@ve). Expect to pay 50-150 EUR.

Booking Flow (Step by Step)

1
Go to the ICP Plus portal

URL: icp.administracionelectronica.gob.es/icpplustiem/citar?p=28 (direct link for Madrid, province 28)

2
Select office and procedure

Office: Cualquier oficina (any office) or pick a specific one.

Procedure (TRAMITES POLICIA NACIONAL): POLICIA-TOMA DE HUELLAS (EXPEDICION DE TARJETA) INICIAL, RENOVACION, DUPLICADO Y LEY 14/2013 (code 4010)

Click Aceptar.

3
Choose "Presentacion con Cl@ve"

This is the path with actual availability. Do NOT choose "sin Cl@ve" -- it will almost certainly show no appointments.

The Cl@ve holder logs in here (this can be a different person than the applicant).

4
Enter the applicant's details

Document type: N.I.E.

NIE number, full name, and nationality of the person who needs the appointment (not the Cl@ve holder).

Click Aceptar.

5
Click "Solicitar Cita"

On the options page, click the red "Solicitar Cita" button. If appointments are available, you'll see a calendar to pick a date and time.

6
Confirm and print

Select your preferred date/time, enter a phone number and email. Confirm the appointment and print the confirmation page. They will not accept a phone screen -- you need a paper copy.

If You Can't Get an Appointment

Manual timing tricks

If you must use the "sin Cl@ve" path, these are the reported best times to check (Madrid):

WhenWhat
Mondays at 12:00Best day/time combo per immigration lawyers
Weekdays at 12:00 and 20:00General slot release windows
8:00 and 14:00Additional release times
Thursdays at 15:00Reported "golden window" by some users

The pre-load trick

Have the form filled out and ready 1 minute before the release time. Refresh at exactly the hour. The browser auto-fills cached fields so you can submit faster than starting fresh.

You won't lose your approval

Don't panic about the 1-month deadline. The regulation says fingerprinting should happen within 1 month, but in 2026 Madrid's extranjeria offices accept justified delays. Take screenshots every time you check and see "no hay citas disponibles" -- these serve as proof you tried. Your authorization stays valid while fingerprinting is pending.

Madrid Office Reference

The system has 40+ offices in Madrid province. When you select "Cualquier oficina" the system checks all of them. Key police stations (CNP) that handle toma de huellas:

  • CNP AVDA POBLADOS -- Avda. de los Poblados, S/N
  • CNP OFICINA AQUILES 2 -- Aquiles, 2
  • CNP SAN FELIPE TIE -- San Felipe, 7
  • CNP Padre Piquer -- Padre Piquer, 18
  • Silva -- Silva, 19 (main Oficina de Extranjeria)
  • Plus 20+ district-level CNP offices and satellite comisarias (Alcobendas, Getafe, Mostoles, etc.)

Note: The system may assign you a specific office based on your empadronamiento address, or it may let you choose from all available offices depending on current configuration.
