🇪🇸 Setting Up Utilities in Madrid

← Main Guide

A practical guide for foreign residents (with NIE) setting up electricity, gas, water, and internet in a Madrid apartment. Written from experience.

Prerequisites: You need a valid NIE and a Spanish bank account (IBAN) before setting up any utility contract. Most providers won't accept a passport alone for ongoing contracts.

Electricity (Luz)

What is CUPS?

Your electricity connection point has a permanent 20-22 character code starting with ES. It never changes when you switch providers. You need this to sign up.

Where to find it: any previous bill for the apartment (ask landlord), or your provider can look it up from the address.

Potencia Contratada (Contracted Power)

This is the maximum power you can draw at once. If you exceed it, the breaker trips.

kWGood forMonthly fixed cost
3.45 kWSmall flat, careful with simultaneous appliances~5/month
4.6 kW RecommendedStandard 65-90m flat with normal appliances~7/month
5.75 kWLarger flat, electric water heater, or heavy simultaneous use~9/month

Start at 4.6 kW. You can change once per year. The 2/month difference isn't worth living with tripped breakers.

Sign Up Process

  1. Get the CUPS code from your landlord or a previous bill
  2. Choose your provider and tariff
  3. Submit online or by phone: NIE, Spanish IBAN, CUPS code, lease contract
  4. Provider handles the switch with the grid distributor (you don't choose the distributor)
  5. Wait 3-5 business days for cambio de titularidad
If the supply was cut off: Reconnection takes 1-5 business days and may require a technician visit. Call the provider immediately on move-in day if there's no power.

Gas

Do You Even Have Gas?

Check your kitchen: gas hob? Look for a white/grey boiler (caldera) on a wall. Many older Madrid buildings have gas for hot water + heating. Newer buildings are increasingly all-electric.

Communal heating (calefaccion central): If your building has communal central heating, you do NOT sign up for individual gas heating. It's included in the community fees. Ask your landlord or portero.

Providers

Naturgy is the dominant gas retailer. Endesa, Iberdrola, and Repsol also offer gas, often bundled with electricity. The distribution network (the actual pipes) is managed by Nedgia (Naturgy group) — you don't choose this.

Sign Up

Same process as electricity: gas CUPS code + NIE + IBAN + lease. Activation takes 5-7 business days. If gas was cut off, a Nedgia technician must inspect the installation before restoring service (can take 5-10 days).

Water (Agua)

Provider: Canal de Isabel II (Only Option)

There is no competition for water in Madrid. Canal de Isabel II is the sole provider.

Sign Up

All you need: NIE + Spanish IBAN + installation address. Sign up online or by phone. A technician comes to install the router and ONT (fiber terminal). You need to be home for the installation.

Book early: Installation appointments can take 7-15 days. Book before you move in, scheduling for your first week in the apartment.

Common Gotchas

Previous tenant didn't cancel their contracts? Not a problem. You don't need them to cancel. Contact any supplier with your NIE, lease contract, and CUPS code and request a cambio de titularidad. Debts before your move-in date are the previous holder's responsibility, not yours. Take meter photos on day one as proof.
  • Gas meter off for a while? Nedgia needs a safety inspection before reconnecting. Budget 5-10 days. Start this early.
  • Potencia too low? If the previous tenant had 2.3 kW and you need 4.6 kW, it costs a small fee and takes a few days. Do it during signup.
  • Communal heating: If your building has calefaccion central, do NOT separately contract individual gas for heating. It's in your community fees.
  • No Spanish bank account yet? Open an N26, Revolut (Spanish IBAN), or Openbank account — takes 10 minutes online. Most utility providers require a Spanish IBAN for direct debit.
  • Movistar promotional prices: They jump significantly after year 1 (from ~20 to ~40/month). Consider switching to O2 or Digi after the promo ends — O2 is literally Movistar's network at a flat price.
